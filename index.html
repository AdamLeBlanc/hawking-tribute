Stephen Hawking

The man who overcame the impossible to open the door to the universe

  • Birth

    Hawking was born on January 8, 1942 to Frank and Isobel Eileen Hawking in Oxford, England. His parents both attended the University of Oxford. He had two younger sisters and an adopted brother.

  • St Albans

    Hwaking's father became the head of a division at the National Institute for Medical Resarch. So the family moved to St Albans, Hertfordshire.

  • School

    Hawking began attending St Albans School. In his first year he was 3rd from the bottom of the class. Instead choosing to focus on pursuits outside of school. This included playing and creating board games, as well as creating computers out of recycled parts for solving basic math problems.

  • Oxford

    Hawking began attending Oxford at the age of 17. He wished to major in mathematics, but as Ocford did not offer this degree he went into Cosmology. He found the work too easy and barley studied, which caused him to have difficulty with "factual" knowledge question, however, he excelled at problem solving.

  • Cambridge

    Hawking graduated with a first-class honours degree from Oxford. Soon after he began his graduate work at Cambridge in October.

  • ALS

    Hawking was dianosed with ALS. He was given two years to live. While his doctors advised him to continue his studies, he felt there was little point. However, it progressed much slower than anticipated and he returned to his studies

  • Engagment

    Hawking beomes engaged to a friend of his sister's, Jane Wilde. Hawking would go on to say the engagment gave him something to live for.

  • Marriage

    The couple got married on July 14th, 1965. Jane lived in London for the first year of their marriage while she completed her degree.

  • Thesis

    Hawking had his thesis approved and recieved his PHD in mathematics and theoretical physics. He based his work on expanding the theory that there is a singularity at the center of a black hole to the entire Universe. At the time there was great debate between two competing theorie on the creation of the Universe: The Big Bang and Steady State theories. Hawkings thesis took the side of the Big Bang, proposing that the Universe started as a singularity.

    After getting his PHD he was given a research fellowship at Cambridge.

  • Robert

    The couples first son, Robert, was born in May.

  • Cambridge

    Hawking's essay, Singularities and the Geometry of Space Time won runner up in the Gravity Research Foundation competition.

  • New Fellowship

    Hawking accepted a specially created fellowship at for distinction in science to remain at cambridge.

  • The proof

    Hawking published proof that if the universe obeys the general theory of relativity and fits any of the models developed by Alexander Friedmann, then it must have started as a singularity.

    Hawking proposes the second law of black hole dynamics, that an event horizon can never get smaller. Along with two other researchers he propsed the four laws of black hole mechanics

  • Lucy

    The couple's second child, a daughty, Lucy, was born.

  • Book #1

    Hawking published his first book: The Large Scale Structure of Space-Time.

  • Hawking Radiation

    Hawking presented his findings on the entropy of black holes. He showed that black hoes emit radiation until they exaust their energy and evaporate. This contradicts his second law of black holes, which is now supersede by this. Eventually this phenomena would become known as Hawking radiation.

    A few weeks a few weeks after presenting Hawking radiation he was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society, at the time the youngest scientist to become a fellow.

  • Lucasian Professor of Mathematics

    Hawking was elected Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at the University of Cambridge. In his first lecture in the role, titled: "Is the End in Sight for Theoretical Physics?", he proposed N=8 Supergravity.

    After a health crisis, Hawking was forced to reluctantly allow some nursing services at his home.

  • Timothy

    The couple's third and final child, a son, Timothy was born in April.

  • The Information Paradox

    Hawking proposes that information in a black hole is lost when a black hole evaporates. This information paradox violates the fundamental tenet of quantum machenaics.

  • Cosmological Inflation

    Hawking organized a workshop on the early universe at Cambridge, mainly focusing on inflation theory.

    Hawking begins a new line of research into the origin of the Universe.

  • No-Boundry Proposal

    Hawking proposed that we live in a none-bound, closed universe. Meaning that prior to the Plank Time at the begining of the Universe, there was space, but no time. Since the universe began as a singularity in both space and time, there were no initial bounds in either for the universe. Therefore the universe has no beginning, since the concept of a beginning without time is meaningless. The singularity is similar to the North Pole. You cannot be more North than the North Pole, but there is also no boundary there. It is simply the point where all paths North meet and end. This model also predicts a closed universe.

  • On Life Support

    During a vist to cern Hawking contracted pneumonia. Due to his condition it was life-threatening. At one point doctors asked Jane if life support should be terminated. She refused, but the illness resulted ina tracheotomy, which required 25/7 care and the removal of what little speaking ability he had left.

    The National Health Service was ready to pay for Hawking to live in a nursing home, but Jane was determined to have her husband live at home. The cost of doing so was paid for by an American Foundation and nurses were hired to cover the 3 shifts required for around the clock care.

  • Getting his voice back

    After initially having to raise his eyebrows to select words on cards to communicate, Hawking was given a computer system he could use to communicate. This was life changing for him, and he proclaimed that he could communicate better with this device than he could before he lost all usage of his voice.

  • A Brief History of Time

    Hawking published his most famous book, A Brief History of Time. The book was aimed at explaining hard science to the general public. Instead of publishing with an academic publisher, he opted for a mass market publisher. The book was a big success, and made it to the top of the best sellers list in many countries. This helped his financial situation greatly, but also brought many challenges that come with being famous. He had little time for work due to an extensive travel schedule, enjoying partying into the early hours of the morning and difficulty refusing invitations or guests.

  • Seperation

    Hawking and Jane seperated afer years of strain on their relationship. Jane had been having an afair with a friend from church, which Hawking was aware of and accpeting of the situation. However he became close with one of his nurses, Elanie Mason. Hawking told Jane he was leaving her for Mason and departed the family home.

  • Second Marriage

    After finalizing his divorce, he married Manson in September. As time went on, family and staff became concerned that Manson was abusing Hawking, but the police closed the investiagtion as Hawking refused to participate in the investigation.

  • Bitter Feelings

    Jane published a memoire, Music to the Stars that went into great detail regarding her marriage to Hawking and their eventual divorce. Some of the revalations in the book caused a media storm, however Hawking, as he always did with his personal life, refused to comment, saying that he does not read biogrophies about himself.

  • The Universe in a Nutshell

    Hawking published what is considered to be a sequal to A Brief History of Time. It was created to update the public on new developments on subjects covered in the first book.

  • Top-Down Cosmology

    Hawking proposes that the universe has many different initial states. It is therefore impossible to predict the current state of the universe based on the initial state. We must instead work backwards from the current state to the initial state, because the current state selects the past states from a superposition of many possible timelines.

  • Divorced... Again

    Hawking quietly ended his second mariage and resumed normal relationships with Jane, his children and the rest of his family. Eventually becoming close once again.

  • Better times

    Reflecting on the better times she spent with Hawking, Jane published a second memoir. This time titled, My Life with Stephen. This would eventually be adapted into the popular film, The Theory of Everything in 2014.

  • A Party for Time Travelers.

    Hawking organized a party, but told no one about it until after it was over so that only time travelers would know about it. Nobody showed up.

  • The Paralympics

    Hawking voiced a segment of the opening cermonies for the paralympics in London.

  • Assisted Suicide

    Hawking publically voiced his support for the legalisation of doctor assisted suicide for the terminally ill.

  • Breakthrough Initiatives

    Hawking helped launch an effort to search for extraterrestial life.

  • Final Interview

    Hawking's final interview, about the detection of gravitational waves, took place in October 2017.

  • Death and Final Paper

    Hawking passed away on Match 14th, 2018, at his home in Cambridge.

    On April 27th, 2018, Hawking's final paper was published, titled A smooth exit from eternal inflation?

    Hawking was born on the 300th anniversay of Galileo's death and died on the 139th anniversary of Einstein's birth.

    He will be interred in the Westminster Abby nave alongside Sir Isaac Newton and near the grave of Charles Darwin on June 15th, 2018.

    He has directed that his epitaph be the Bakesnstein-Hawking entrophy equation.
